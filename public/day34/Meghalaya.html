Meghalaya Adventures

Exploring the Abode of Clouds

Caves, Forests & Living Roots

Meghalaya, known as the "Abode of Clouds," offers thrilling adventures including jungle trekking, spelunking through ancient caves, and witnessing the awe-inspiring living root bridges.

Adventure Highlights

Living Root Bridges

Marvel at the handmade bridges grown from tree roots deep in the forest.

×

Living Root Bridges – 5 Days

Description: Trek to the double-decker living root bridge in Nongriat, a surreal example of bioengineering by Khasi tribes.

Itinerary:

  • Day 1: Drive to Tyrna, trek to Nongriat, settle into homestay, and explore the village
  • Day 2: Visit Rainbow Falls, enjoy a refreshing dip, and return to Nongriat.
  • Day 3: Trek to Ka Ri Tymmen Root Bridge, witness Khasi bioengineering, and explore Umkyrshan Steel Suspension Bridge. -
  • Day 4: Relax at Umshong Natural Swimming Pool, enjoy a fish manicure, and interact with locals. -
  • - Day 5: Trek back to Tyrna, drive to Shillong, and conclude the journey.

Inclusions:

  • Local guide for navigation and insights into Khasi traditions.
  • Homestay with traditional Khasi hospitality
  • Meals featuring local delicacies
  • Equipment for trekking.

Mawlynnong & Dawki

Visit Asia's cleanest village and boat on crystal-clear river waters.

×

Mawlynnong & Dawki – 5 Days

Description: A scenic journey covering Mawlynnong – the cleanest village in Asia, and boating in the emerald waters of Dawki river.

Itinerary:

  • Day 1: Drive from Shillong, visit Lapynshongdor viewpoint, and explore Mawlynnong village.
  • Day 2: Visit the 100-year-old Epiphany Church and trek to Riwai Living Root Bridge.
  • Day 3: Drive by Borhill Falls and Umkrem Falls, then head to Dawki.
  • Day 4: Enjoy boating on the crystal-clear Umngot River and visit Tamabil Zero Point.
  • Day 5: Explore Byrdaw Falls and Rymbai Falls before returning to Shillong.

Inclusions:

  • Private cab for comfortable travel.
  • Experienced guide for insights into local culture.
  • Lunch featuring authentic Khasi cuisine.
  • Entry fees for attractions.
  • Boating experience on Umngot River.
  • Safety equipment (life jackets, first aid kit).

Siju Cave Exploration

Explore ancient limestone caves with underground river systems.

×

Siju Cave Exploration – 5 Days

Description: Journey into Meghalaya's hidden underworld of stalactites, cave fish, and echo chambers at Siju Cave.

Itinerary:

  • Day 1: Drive from Shillong to Siju, settle into camp, and explore the cave entrance.
  • Day 2: Begin deep cave exploration, witness stunning stalactites and underground streams.
  • Day 3: Visit Siju Bird Sanctuary, spot migratory birds, and trek to nearby rock formations.
  • Day 4: Explore hidden chambers, experience the echo phenomenon, and relax by Simsang River.
  • Day 5: Trek back to base, enjoy a farewell meal, and return to Shillong.

Inclusions:

  • Professional caving gear for safe exploration.
  • Experienced guide and porters for navigation.
  • Meals and camp stays in scenic locations.
  • Entry fees for caves and sanctuary.
  • Safety equipment (helmets, torches, first aid kit).

Laitlum Canyon Trek

Hike to a breathtaking canyon known as the ‘End of the Hills’.

×

Laitlum Canyon – 5 Days

Description: A multi-day trek through Laitlum Canyon, exploring breathtaking landscapes, hidden trails, and Khasi village life.

Itinerary:

  • Day 1: Drive from Shillong to Laitlum Canyon, enjoy panoramic views, and set up camp.
  • Day 2: Trek through scenic trails, visit Rasong village, and interact with locals.
  • Day 3: Explore hidden waterfalls and caves, enjoy a picnic at a scenic viewpoint.
  • Day 4: Hike to nearby ridges, witness sunrise and sunset over the canyon.
  • Day 5: Relax at the canyon edge, trek back to base, and return to Shillong.

Inclusions:

  • Experienced guide for navigation and cultural insights.
  • Camping gear and accommodations in scenic locations.
  • Meals featuring local Khasi cuisine.
  • Photography assistance to capture stunning landscapes.
  • Entry fees for attractions and trekking permits.
  • Safety equipment (first aid kit, trekking poles, emergency supplies).
